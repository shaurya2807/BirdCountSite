About

Bird Count is an initiative by Kushiluv and Abhinn, students at IIIT-Delhi, undertaken with the support of Prof. Pankaj Jalote and Prof. Saket Anand. Our project integrates the latest advancements in deep learning with ecological monitoring techniques. It's designed to address the challenges of manual bird counting in large flocks, a task that is both time-consuming and prone to inaccuracies. By developing an automated system, Bird Count aims to revolutionize how bird populations are studied and tracked, providing reliable data for ecological research and conservation policies.

Our Objective and Your Participation

Our primary goal with Bird Count is to offer a precise and user-friendly platform for accurately monitoring bird populations. By harnessing the power of deep learning, we seek to automate the counting process, thereby increasing efficiency for researchers and conservationists. This technology is designed to minimize errors and provide rapid, reliable data to support wildlife studies and habitat management. In pursuit of enhancing our model's accuracy, Bird Count invites users to actively participate by annotating images where the automated count may fall short. These user-generated annotations are crucial; they not only help in refining the algorithm but also ensure the system continuously learns and improves. By involving users in this feedback loop, we foster a community-driven approach to data collection, ultimately leading to more robust environmental research tools.
